The Unusual Life of Judit Polgar

  • 1976 - is born.
  • 1986 - defeats 52-year-old Romanian IM Dolfi Drimer in the Adsteam Lidums International Tournament in Adelaide, Australia.
  • 1988 - made her first International Master norm in the International B section of the New York Open.
  • 1989 - rated 2555, which was number 55 in the world and 35 rating points ahead of the Women's World Champion Maia Chiburdanidze.
  • 1991 - achieved the grandmaster title by winning the Hungarian National Championship, at the time the youngest ever at 15 years, 5 months to have achieved the title. This beat Bobby Fischer's record by a month.
  • 1993 - Bobby Fischer ducks a match against her with dubious logic, tarnishing his legacy forever.
  • 1996 - becomes the only woman ever to be ranked in the top ten of all chess players.
  • 1998 - defeats World Champion Anatoly Karpov in an eight-game match.
  • 1998 - becomes the first woman to ever qualify for the US Open
  • 2000 - wins one of the strongest tournaments ever held in Asia.
